Contributions:

User Research, Product Design, Graphic Design, Webdesign

Client:

Mater Transformation Team

Sector:

Patient Education

Duration:

6 Weeks

Aim

Promoting dialogue about rising levels within coastal communities in order to increase awareness of the possibility of climate adaptation measures.

Key Research Insights

Patient Interviews
  • Patients reported being unclear on the hospital transition timeline and did not how know the adult care how it would differ from the paediatrics.
  • Patients did not know of all service available to them in the adult metabolic clinic, such as what a medical social worker does.
  • Some patients feel as if they know their blood levels result will be negative after not adhering to their diet, so they put it off.
    • Staff Interviews
  • “ A gap I see at the minute is that we have nothing in writing.” - Metabolic Dietician
  • Patients are supposed to send in their blood tests every month, but this very rarely happens.
  • The patients who need dietary support the most, call the dietician support line the least.

    • Empathising with the User

    From insights gathered through patient interviews, I created three user personas to help me better understand their needs, motivations, and pain points patients may experience after the transition process.

    The committed patient

    Values their health and strives to achieve the best possible outcomes for their condition.

    The average patient

    Understands the importance of managing their condition but may face challenges in adhering to treatment routine.

    The struggling patient

    Faced challenges during the transition resulting in low adherence. Has a lack of knowledge of services available to them.

    Experience Map

    A product-based journey map helped to better understand the course phases as well as the user and their experience in them. The originally planned syllabus was greatly adapted as there were unnecessary and unfavorable steps coming to light.
